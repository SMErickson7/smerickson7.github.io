Strava

I started cycling in the summer of 2018 after a couple of friends talked about their rides together. For 20+ years I played soccer to keep the extra pounds off, but it was taking a toll on my body. I was looking for a new, fun, and low impact activity. And if I could do it with my friends, even better. I didn't know much about cycling (I did know how to ride a bike), so through the advice of my close friends, I was able to purchase my first road bike, acquire the right gear, and download Strava.

Strava is a social network for athletes and weekend warriors to track, measure, analyze, and share their physical exercise activities. Their platform has over 30 types of activities and its social network features has created the largest sports community in the world. It is the leading service to record and upload cycling rides through its mobile app or via a third-party GPS bike computer. Each ride records time, speed, distance, and elevation. Additional features, like user-generated “segments”, brings a competitive spirit to the social networking side of Strava.

As of November, 2022, I have used Strava to track 149 bike rides. Throughout the last four years, a list of pain points has been compiled via personal experience and conversation with my friends who use Strava.

Additionally, there are features that I believe would enhance the experience
  • Designating checkpoints
  • Dark mode

Experience during an activity

Current experience with Strava
Pain-points
  • Switching screens during a ride to see different metrics

    • To see speed, distance traveled, or time elapsed, a user has to tap the small icon in the bottom right. When traveling at speed, a user has to shift their focus from the road to tapping the small icon, which is dangerous.

  • Button size and placement

    • As mentioned above, the icon to switch between map and metrics view is small. Additionally the cluster of icons on the map (2D/3D view, center location, and layers) are too close together. The layout shows that Strava did not consider certain use cases of their app being used during an activity. Many users will use a cell phone mount, which makes the outer edge of the screen not useable. In colder months, cyclists will wear full fingertip gloves. Tapping one of these buttons with accuracy in these use cases is difficult.

  • Route visibility

    • Strava uses two shades of blue to visualize the route and the user's progress. If the user is riding a route that crosses itself, the cognitive load to determine which direction to go could lead to confusion and an indecisive cyclist puts his/her safety in danger.

  • Map doesn't auto-rotate to user's direction of travel

    • In map view, the map does not rotate in the user's direction of travel. The map keeps north up. For some users left and right turns require a moment of mental effort before turning. This as well could lead to confusion and an indecisive cyclist puts his/her safety in danger.

    Update:
    Strava solved this in summer of 2022. However, when the ride is auto-paused (feature by Strava that pauses a ride when GPS movement has stopped), the compass will default back to "north up". The user has to tap the compass icon to change to "auto-rotate".

Activity redesign

In my redesign, I addressed the pain -points with the existing map view. Also I added two new features to enhance the experience during an activity.

Secondary view options

Moved secondary view options to top left to avoid accidental selection. Additionally increased the size and padding of the icons.

Auto-rotate

Auto-rotate was moved to top right, increased size and padding. The icon was changed to a universal style icon that would be familiar to users.

Checkpoint New

While drawing a route, I would add a feature that allows the user to designate a waypoint on the route as a "checkpoint". The checkpoint would be visable on the map during an activity. This would be beneficial to users who divide their rides into segments and would use checkpoints as mile markers or breaks.

Route color

Route color was changed to blue and orange for greater discrepancy. Blue represents the part of the route completed, orange represents part of the route not completed.

Location icon

Location icon now stands alone at the bottom right of the map. The icon is larger and not as close to the edge of the screen.

Ride metrics New

Ride metrics are now visible on the map view. This is a new view and displays the average speed, current speed, time elapsed, and distance. For rides that are using a route, the distance will show the progress (distance traveled and total distance). For rides that are not using a route, distance traveled will be displayed. I made the icon to switch to metrics view larger and changed to a speedometer.

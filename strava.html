Strava

I started cycling in the summer of 2018 after a couple of friends talked about their rides together. For 20+ years I played soccer to keep the extra pounds off, but it was taking a toll on my body. I was looking for a new, fun, and low impact activity. And if I could do it with my friends, even better. I didn't know much about cycling (I did know how to ride a bike), so through the advice of my close friends, I was able to purchase my first road bike, acquire the right gear, and download Strava.

Strava is a social network for athletes and weekend warriors to track, measure, analyze, and share their physical exercise activities. Their platform has over 30 types of activities and its social network features has created the largest sports community in the world. It is the leading service to record and upload cycling rides through its mobile app or via a third-party GPS bike computer. Each ride records time, speed, distance, and elevation. Additional features, like user-generated “segments”, brings a competitive spirit to the social networking side of Strava.

As of November, 2022, I have used Strava to track 149 bike rides. Throughout the last four years, a list of pain points has been compiled via personal experience and conversation with my friends who use Strava.

Pain points
  • Switching screens during a ride to see different metrics
  • Map doesn't auto-rotate to my direction of travel (Strava solved this in summer of 2022)
  • Button size and placement
  • Route visibility
Additionally, there are features that I believe would enhance the experience
  • Creating checkpoints
  • Dark mode
